What your reviewer looks at.

The same four lenses a hiring panel uses — applied to 1–2 of your case studies.

Storytelling

Does your case study read as a decision narrative, or a screenshot gallery? Structure, flow, and what to cut.

UX decision-making

Can a reader trace why you made each call — and would you survive the "why this, not that?" question live?

Business impact

What changed because of your work? Framing outcomes credibly, even without perfect metrics.

Visual hierarchy

Whether the craft of the portfolio itself — layout, emphasis, scannability — works for a 90-second skim.

How the two weeks run.

Questions, answered.

What does it cost?
Nothing. The sprint is free, run by volunteer mentors from the community.
What do I need to apply?
A portfolio with at least 3 case studies. The review focuses deeply on 1–2 of them.
Who are the mentors?
Practicing UX and product designers with 4+ years of experience, matched to your target roles where possible.
How is this different from a Discord critique?
Channel critiques are quick takes. The sprint is a structured hiring-style review with written feedback, your revision, and a follow-up session to validate it.
